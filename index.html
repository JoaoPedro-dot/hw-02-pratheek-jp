Task Abstraction

High-Level: Consume -> Discover

  • We are meant to consume the visualization, not produce anything. We are learning new information that was not previously evident from the data

Mid-Level: Search -> Lookup

  • Given the data for "Last 3 days" are highlighted, we know our target and want to know about its location

Low-Level: Query -> Summarize

  • This is a query meant to summarize the data because we are looking at the entirety of the visualization, seeing all the data at once

Target: All Data -> Trends

  • The visualization has all of the data and shows a trend of daily maximum temperatures from 1979-2021 in Vancouver

High-Level: Consume -> Present

  • In this visualization, the reader is consuming data that is being presented to them. The information could be used to communicate to a meteorologist identifying outliers in temperature data and wanted a visual representation.

Mid-Level: Search -> Browse

  • This is a browse because we know the rough estimate but not the exact location of the data. We want to observe temperatures above 20 degrees F

Low-Level: Query -> Compare

  • We are comparing the daily mean temperatures above 20 degrees F between Seattle and Portland, looking for similarities and differences

Target: Attributes -> Distribution

  • We want to look for patterns in the data and anomolies such as outliers
